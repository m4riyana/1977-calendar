Rizal Day is a national holiday in the Philippines observed every December 30. It commemorates the martyrdom of Dr. José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines, who was executed by Spanish authorities in Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park) in 1896.

Rizal’s writings and sacrifice inspired Filipinos to unite and fight for independence. Every year, ceremonies are held at the Rizal Monument in Luneta, Manila, and across the country to honor his legacy of nationalism, courage, and patriotism.
